Client Onboarding Checklist

Week 1 Discovery + Access
Send welcome email with onboarding overview and what to expect
Schedule 60-min discovery call (use Calendly or direct link)
Collect access credentials: CRM, email platform, helpdesk, social accounts
Document current workflows — map the exact manual process you're replacing
Identify #1 bottleneck to solve first (start narrow, win fast)
Confirm tools, APIs, and integration points needed
Send Week 1 summary and confirm build scope in writing
Send to client — End of Week 1
Hey [Name], great kickoff call. Here's a quick summary of what we're building: [1-2 sentence scope]. We have everything we need to start the build. You'll see the first workflow live by [date]. I'll check in midweek — let me know if anything comes up before then.
Week 2 Build + Launch
Build first workflow in your agent platform (n8n, Make, custom, etc.)
Connect all integrations and test data flow end-to-end
Run 10+ test cases covering edge cases (empty inputs, errors, timeouts)
Soft launch: run live with limited volume (10-20% of real traffic)
Monitor outputs daily — catch issues before they compound
Document the workflow logic (client-facing one-pager)
Send to client — End of Week 2
Hey [Name], your first workflow is live. Here's what's running: [brief description]. We're at limited volume right now to make sure everything looks clean. Early output is attached. I'll have a full review ready by end of next week — if you spot anything in the meantime, hit me directly.
Week 3 Review + Optimize
Pull first week of live data — output volume, quality, error rate
Identify top 3 improvement areas from real-world performance
Tune prompts, rules, or logic based on findings
Scale to full traffic volume once quality bar is confirmed
Build the first KPI report (baseline metrics, what changed, what's next)
Schedule Week 4 handoff call
Send to client — End of Week 3
Hey [Name], first optimization done. Here's what we saw in week one: [2-3 data points]. Here's what I changed and why: [brief]. We're now at full volume. KPI report attached. Our handoff call is [date/time] — we'll walk through this together and set the ongoing reporting rhythm.
Week 4 Handoff to Ongoing
Deliver Month 1 results summary (vs. baseline, ROI calculation if possible)
Walk client through the workflow on a screen share — demystify what's running
Confirm ongoing reporting cadence (weekly / monthly) and format
Set next 30-day optimization priorities with the client
Confirm recurring billing is active
Ask for a referral or testimonial (timing is perfect — momentum is high)
Send to client — End of Week 4
Hey [Name], that's Month 1 wrapped. Here's the summary: [headline result]. Your workflows are running clean and the next optimization cycle starts [date]. One ask — if you know anyone else who'd benefit from this, I'd appreciate the intro. Talk soon.

The 30-Second Positioning Script

01 Networking Event — Casual
I help businesses automate the manual work that's eating their team's time — things like lead follow-up, customer support, and internal workflows. We basically set up AI agents that handle it all so the team can focus on the work that actually moves revenue. What do you do?
2 sentences. No jargon. Works at a dinner table or a conference floor.
02 Sales Call Opener — Formal
Thanks for taking the time. Quick context on what we do: we build and manage AI agent systems for businesses that are losing revenue to manual processes they haven't had time to fix. Most of our clients come to us because they're growing, but their operations aren't keeping pace. I read through your site before the call — it looks like you're doing [X]. I'm curious, where's the biggest bottleneck right now?
Establishes credibility, shows prep, and opens discovery without sounding scripted. Swap [X] for something real.
03 Cold DM — Written
Hey [Name] — I help [their niche] automate [specific manual task] so they can stop losing [time/leads/revenue] to it. Built something similar for [type of business]. Would a 15-minute call be worth it to show you what we built?
Under 50 words. One clear CTA. The specificity of "their niche" and "specific task" is what gets the reply — keep the brackets filled in before sending.
